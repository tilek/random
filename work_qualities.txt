This is a selection of work qualities I believe people could have.
I find this list useful for finding and evaluating people to collaborate with.


Examples of important areas missing from this list: 
- general intellectual abilities (e.g. problem solving, creativity)
- domain-specific skills (e.g. engineering)
- language skills (e.g. English grammar)
- knowledge of concepts and facts


1. Preferences.

1.1. Collaboration preferences

- Bias to action
- Enjoyment of helping people
- Fair credit assignment
- Fairness
- Leadership (taking matters in your own hands)
- Ownership (acting as an owner)
- Reliability (helping others rely on them)

1.2. Communication preferences

- Approachability
- Authenticity
- Gratefulness
- Honesty
- Humbleness
- Receptiveness to feedback
- Transparency

1.3. Learning preferences

- Curiosity
- Enjoyment of learning new skills
- Enjoyment of learning new concepts
- Enjoyment of diving into challenges

1.4. Motivation

- Determination (drive to overcome barriers to succeed)
- Drive to set new challenges & self-improve

1.5. Other preferences

- Ethicality
- Frugality
- Openness to challenging own ideas
- Positivity
- Proactiveness
- Pursuit of quality
- Taking responsibility for failures


2. Abilities.

2.1. Collaboration skills

- Ability to work independently
- Ability to perform well under pressure

2.2. Communication skills

- Ability to articulate a compelling vision
- Ability to come up with good jokes
- Ability to communicate well during stressful situations
- Ability to convince people
- Ability to explain complex concepts simply
- Ability to explain thoughts well verbally
- Ability to explain thoughts well in writing
- Ability to improve someone's mood
- Ability to make a random person like you
- Ability to provide good personal development feedback
- Ability to tell a compelling story

2.3. Learning skills

- Ability to learn complex concepts quickly
- Ability to learn new skills quickly
- Ability to learn new skills independently
- Ability to think critically

2.4. Productivity skills

- Ability to choose goals rationally
- Ability to make good decisions quickly
- Ability to focus on a task
- Ability to focus on a long-term goal
- Ability to plan time well

2.5. Resourcefulness, discipline, resilience, and grit

- Ability to find clever ways to overcome challenges
- Ability to gain extra motivation from challenging events
- Ability to manage own mood during challenging events
- Ability to quickly recover from challenging events
- Discipline to complete unpleasant work to achieve set goals
- Discipline to make stable progress
- Discipline to meet commitments or inform otherwise

2.6. Self-improvement skills

- Ability to build new good habits
- Ability to stop bad habits
- Ability to quickly self-improve
